About K-Pop

K-pop, or Korean Pop, is a musical genre from South Korea that blends styles like pop, hip-hop, and electronic music, standing out for its choreographed performances and elaborate visual production. It emerged in the 1990s with Seo Taiji and Boys, who revolutionized Korean music by incorporating Western influences and addressing social issues. Since then, companies like SM, YG, and JYP Entertainment have developed rigorous training systems to debut *idols*. Groups like BTS, BLACKPINK, and EXO have brought K-pop to global audiences, especially from the 2010s onwards, fueled by social media and platforms like YouTube. More than just music, K-pop has become a cultural phenomenon, influencing fashion, behavior, and promoting South Korean culture worldwide. Today, it continues to grow, with new generations of artists and fans spreading its reach across the globe.
